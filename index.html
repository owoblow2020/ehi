SCHOOL EYEHEALTH PROGRAM: SCHOOL-AGED CHILDREN GETS SCREENED

Eyesavers Healthcare Initiative provided a comprehensive eyecare services to school-aged children between the ages of 4 to 9. Through systematic screening, testing, and medication provision,with reading glasses included. The aim was to ensure that these children enjoy equal privileges of good eyesight and better academic performance.

A total of 67 school-aged children were screened, ensuring comprehensive coverage of the target population. 23 children were identified with visual impairments or eye conditions, highlighting the importance of early intervention. All identified cases received appropriate medications and treatments, addressing their specific needs and improving their overall eye health. Through this systematic approach, we are confident that these children will enjoy improved eyesight, leading to enhanced academic performance and better opportunities for success

Mr. Kayode, The School Van Driver

Mr. Kayode, a school van driver, lived with increasing worries as he went about his daily activities. While driving, he often experienced sharp pains in his eyes and tearing, especially when exposed to oncoming vehicle lights. These symptoms caused him to lose coordination, posing a serious concern as he transported school-aged children. His anxiety peaked until Eyesavers Healthcare Initiative organized a sensitization seminar through its School Eyehealth Initiative. Mr. Kayode promptly participated in the questionnaire distributed during the seminar. Following his responses, he was directed to our partnering Eyecare Center, where he received free eye care services including screening, medication, and medicated eyeglasses. Filled with relief and excitement, Mr. Kayode expressed deep appreciation to Eyesavers Healthcare Initiative and its supporters. He was overwhelmed with gratitude for the prompt response to his needs and the opportunity to receive free eye care services. With a renewed sense of hope, he eagerly embraced the chance to improve his vision and ensure the safety of his passengers, especially the school-aged children he transported daily. Mr. Kayode's heartfelt thanks echoed the sentiment of countless others who had benefited from the initiative's compassion and commitment to enhancing eye health in the community.

Abuchi Community Outreach; CHildren Not Left Behind

In Abuchi Village, children's health was carefully managed: First, they formed a line outside the clinic, under the guidance of healthcare workers. Then, each child underwent deworming treatment, tailored to their needs, inside the clinic. They were also educated on hygiene practices to prevent future infections. After treatment, ongoing monitoring and follow-up appointments were scheduled. Finally, with treatment complete, children celebrated their renewed health and vitality.
